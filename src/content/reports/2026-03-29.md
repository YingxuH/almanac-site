### The Futures Diff
Yesterday, the narrative was dominated by the shift to cloud-hosted frontier models and "Kitchen Loop" orchestration. Today, we are seeing the direct operational blowback of that shift. Cloud reliance means rationing; developers hitting the wall on Claude Opus (5 messages/5 hours) are suddenly realizing that decoupled hardware has a massive hidden cost in API throttling. 

This friction is accelerating two specific trends:
1. **The Local Orchestration Rebound:** Tools like OpenYak are gaining immediate traction not out of ideological purity, but practical necessity. If the cloud caps your workflow, you move the orchestrator locally.
2. **The "AI DevOps" Wall:** We are seeing the first coordinated cultural resistance from SREs and Platform Engineers. The initial euphoria of "AI writing code" is crashing into the rigid reality of "AI breaking production," pivoting enterprise focus from *generation* to *verification*.
