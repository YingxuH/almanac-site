# Almanac Daily Forecast — 2026-03-29
## The Future of Software Engineering

### Executive Summary
Following yesterday's realization that local compute is dead with the discontinuation of the Mac Pro, today reveals the psychological toll of infinite cloud compute on the human developer. A severe wave of "Fear of Missing Code" (FOMC) and "vibe coding" fatigue has crashed into the industry, tanking workflow sentiment as always-on AI assistants disrupt sleep cycles and generate unmanageable review debt. Meanwhile, a massive reallocation of frontier compute is underway: OpenAI officially killed its Sora video model to focus on profitability and core reasoning, just as open-source communities and startups debut pure-AI operating systems and autonomous research hubs.

### Today's Top Signals

**1. The "Pure AI" Operating System Materializes**
The release of Pneuma—a desktop computing environment with zero pre-installed applications—demonstrates a radical shift in software delivery. Users boot to a prompt and describe the tool they need, prompting an LLM to generate a self-contained Rust module compiled to WebAssembly in under a second. This moves the paradigm past "agents writing files" to ephemeral, on-demand software generation that persists only as long as the user needs it. *(Source: pneuma.computer)*

**2. OpenAI Kills Sora, Pivots Compute to Core Reasoning**
OpenAI abruptly scrapped its Sora video-generation app, cancelled a $1B Disney deal, and halted plans for video inside ChatGPT. As the company angles for a massive 2026 IPO (backed by a newly reported $40B SoftBank loan), this signals that flashy, high-compute generative media is being ruthlessly culled. Frontier compute is being strictly rationed for high-margin, high-ROI enterprise reasoning and coding tasks. *(Source: The Verge, TechCrunch)*

**3. "Fear of Missing Code" (FOMC) Triggers Severe Burnout**
The reality of 24/7 autonomous coding is destroying developer sleep cycles. A viral discussion highlights developers staying up late to ensure their "Claude Code Max" subscriptions are running optimally while they sleep or eat. Coupled with rising disillusionment over "vibe coding"—where models exhibit "yes-man" behavior but deliver incomplete, restrictive architectures—the adoption gap between enthusiastic executives and exhausted individual contributors (ICs) is widening rapidly. *(Source: Hacker News, John J. Wang)*

**4. The Real-Time AI Interview Crisis**
Hiring managers are reporting a flood of candidates for senior engineering roles who are visibly reading from real-time AI prompts during technical interviews. The speed, unbroken verbosity, and multi-point rationale of these AI-assisted answers are forcing hiring committees to instantly reject candidates based on eye-tracking and conversational cadence, signaling a breakdown in traditional technical vetting. *(Source: Hacker News)*

**5. Autonomous Open-Source "Scientists" Challenge Corporate Labs**
Sakana AI released AI-Scientist-v2 (featuring workshop-level automated discovery via agentic tree search), while the open-source community launched Enlidea, a decentralized hub for machine-to-machine research. By allowing autonomous agents to propose hypotheses, stake bounties, and execute code, the open-source community is actively preempting the closed-door automated researchers promised by major labs. *(Source: GitHub, Enlidea)*

**6. AI and Proof Assistants Solve Knuth's "Claude Cycles"**
A combination of human steering, Large Language Models, and formal proof assistants has successfully solved Donald Knuth's "Claude Cycles" problem. This milestone proves that AI coding tools are breaking past standard web boilerplate and are now actively expanding the frontier of complex computer science mathematics and formal logic proofs. *(Source: Hacker News / Twitter)*
