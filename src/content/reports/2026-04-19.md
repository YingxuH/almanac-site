### Executive Summary
Yesterday's structural cracks in vulnerability management are today giving way to localized friction in both economics and education. Meanwhile, sustained negative sentiment in the skills domain highlights a growing educational backlash, with some institutions actively regressing to analog tools to combat AI, creating a widening gap between academic computer science and modern engineering realities. 

### Today's Top Signals

**1. Opus 4.7 Token Inflation Reaches ~45%**
Following yesterday's early signals of economic friction, token tracking leaderboards today revealed that the tokenization inflation from Anthropic's Claude 4.6 to 4.7 sits at roughly 45% ([tokens.billchambers.me](https://tokens.billchambers.me/leaderboard)). As developers use the same prompts but consume significantly more tokens, the effective cost of running autonomous coding agents is secretly ballooning, presenting a massive economic hurdle for scaling AI workflows.

**2. Academic Backlash Forces Regression to Analog**
In a stark reflection of our negative sentiment tracker for the "skills" domain, a college instructor has made headlines for forcing students to use typewriters to curb AI-written work ([sentinelcolorado.com](https://sentinelcolorado.com/uncategorized/a-college-instructor-turns-to-typewriters-to-curb-ai-written-work-and-teach-life-lessons/)). This extreme reversion highlights a systemic institutional failure to adapt curricula to a generative AI world, suggesting traditional computer science and software education pipelines are breaking under the pressure of new paradigms.

**3. WireGuard 1.0 Released for Windows**
In a major milestone for core infrastructure, WireGuard 1.0 has officially been released for Windows ([lore.kernel.org](https://lore.kernel.org/wireguard/CAHmME9pDd2JMcEuSgOKpXPhUB8FSO+rNJdTkXRzpLhK1_xW9Cg@mail.gmail.com/)). This native kernel integration enables high-performance, secure networking directly within the Windows OS ecosystem. It serves as a stark reminder that while AI dominates the headlines, foundational security and networking protocols are still being painstakingly advanced by human engineers.

**4. MDV and the Evolution of Developer Workflows**
A new Markdown superset called MDV launched today, specifically designed for docs, dashboards, and slides with integrated data ([github.com/drasimwagan/mdv](https://github.com/drasimwagan/mdv)). Concurrently, the community is actively dissecting the workflow implications of "Claude Design" ([samhenri.gold](https://samhenri.gold/blog/20260418-claude-design/)). These incremental releases are driving a positive sentiment (+0.10) in our workflow domain, proving developers are highly focused on integrating AI with structured, lightweight text formats rather than abandoning them.

**5. Database Architecture Reality Checks**
A severe PostgreSQL production incident caused by a transaction ID wraparound was detailed today ([sqlservercentral.com](https://www.sqlservercentral.com/articles/i-too-have-a-production-story-a-downtime-caused-by-postgres-transaction-id-wraparound-problem)), occurring alongside the launch of PgQue, a zero-bloat queue system built directly on Postgres ([github.com/NikolayS/pgque](https://github.com/NikolayS/pgque)). These signals confirm that deep, system-level understanding of database internals remains critical, defying the narrative that AI will abstract away the need for senior infrastructure oversight.

**6. Cloud Migration Driven by Cost**
We are tracking an uptick in engineers migrating infrastructure from DigitalOcean to Hetzner to optimize server costs ([isayeter.com](https://isayeter.com/posts/digitalocean-to-hetzner-migration/)). This developer-led penny-pinching underscores the broader economic tightening across the software sector, directly competing with the capital demands of heavy AI tooling.
