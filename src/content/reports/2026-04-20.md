# Almanac Daily Forecast — 2026-04-20
## The Future of Software Engineering

### Executive Summary
The structural security cracks we identified yesterday are deteriorating into a full-blown infrastructure crisis today, evidenced by a cluster of high-profile vulnerabilities spanning Vercel, Notion, and Protobuf. Concurrently, the economic friction of enterprise AI is colliding with a hard performance ceiling; despite continued optimism around consumer-grade "vibe-coding," tech giants like Uber are hitting a $3.4B wall in AI spending. This budget fatigue is severely compounded by alarming, measurable coding regressions and systemic "token inflation" in Anthropic's newest Claude Opus 4.7 model, fundamentally challenging the narrative of exponential, cost-free AI capability gains.

### Today's Top Signals

**1. Claude Opus 4.7 Demonstrates Measurable Coding Regression & "Token Inflation"**
User session data comparing Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 to the newly deployed 4.7 reveals a startling degradation in coding capabilities. The one-shot success rate dropped from 83.8% (v4.6) to just 74.5% (v4.7), while retries per edit more than doubled from 0.22 to 0.46. Crucially, v4.7 produces roughly 800 tokens per call compared to 372 in v4.6, driving the cost per call up from $0.112 to $0.185. This perfectly encapsulates the "token inflation" dynamic: models are getting more verbose to mask declining zero-shot reasoning capabilities. *(Source: Hacker News user telemetry)*

**2. Uber's Enterprise AI Budget Hits a $3.4 Billion Wall**
Uber's CTO publicly acknowledged profound budget struggles regarding the company's AI scaling push, despite a staggering $3.4B spend. As enterprise companies attempt to move AI coding tools from isolated pilot programs into standardized, company-wide infrastructure, the combined costs of compute, API calls, and human review are straining traditional engineering budgets. *(Source: Yahoo Finance)*

**3. Infrastructure Security Deteriorates: Vercel, Notion, and Protobuf**
A trifecta of severe security incidents hit the ecosystem today. Vercel confirmed an April 2026 data breach following hacker claims of stolen data, Notion leaked email addresses for public page editors, and a critical flaw in the widespread Protobuf library was found to enable arbitrary JavaScript code execution. This drives our security domain sentiment to a deeply negative -0.80, indicating that rapid iteration cycles are outpacing governance and QA. *(Source: BleepingComputer, Twitter/X)*

**4. The Polarization of "Vibe-Coding": Individual Enablement vs. Institutional Slop**
A developer successfully built "Faceoff"—a Python-based terminal UI app for NHL games requiring the `uv` tool—relying almost entirely on Claude Code to "vibe-code" the product. While an open-source mailing list paradoxically claimed "the time when we suffer from large amounts of AI slop is gone," this solo-developer success stands in stark contrast to the enterprise struggles noted at Uber. AI is succeeding as a prototyping tool for individuals but struggling as a scalable, maintainable infrastructure for teams. *(Source: vincentgregoire.com, haxx.se)*

**5. Governance Strains in Open Source and Fast-Moving Teams**
Structural strains in engineering collaboration are worsening. Matt Mullenweg unilaterally overruled Core Committers to mandate Akismet on the WordPress 7 Connector List, underscoring top-down friction in OSS governance. Simultaneously, industry commentary highlighting that "when moving fast, talking is the first thing to break" reflects a growing workflow crisis where AI-accelerated code generation is destroying necessary human communication loops. *(Source: The Repository, daverupert.com)*

**6. Decentralized AI Execution: Gemma 4 in the Browser**
A new prompt-to-Excalidraw demo leveraging Gemma 4 and E2B successfully ran entirely in the browser via WebAssembly (requiring a 3.1GB download). This signals an accelerating shift toward local, client-side execution of AI tooling, pushing back against the expensive, centralized API dependency represented by OpenAI and Anthropic. *(Source: GitHub Pages/TeamChong)*
