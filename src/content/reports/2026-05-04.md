*Why it matters for the 3-30 year outlook:* AI coding assistants are aggressively breaking out of the IDE. By wiring standard agentic protocols (like MCP) into non-textual, highly complex creative software, developers are laying the groundwork for true multimodal AI workflows where agents manipulate audio, video, and 3D states directly. 

**4. The Resurgence of TUIs and High-Control Environments**
Developer discourse today heavily indexed on a return to Text User Interfaces (TUIs) and the repurposing of highly deterministic development tools—such as using Emacs, HTML, and shell scripts to author and publish novels.
*Why it matters for the 3-30 year outlook:* As high-level web frameworks and AI agents abstract away traditional coding, a growing counter-culture of experienced engineers is seeking refuge in ultra-customizable, text-based workflows. This polarization suggests the future of developer tooling will bifurcate between "vibe coding" abstraction layers and hyper-deterministic, low-level environments.

**5. Systems Engineering Endurance: RISC-V Emulators and 'C'**
A developer successfully built a RISC-V emulator from scratch, implementing a minimal syscall interface via `newlib` to run DOOM using native 'C' compilation.
*Why it matters for the 3-30 year outlook:* High-level application development is rapidly succumbing to AI automation, but low-level systems architecture—emulators, kernels, and bare-metal compilation—remains highly resistant to LLM hallucination. The enduring reverence for these fundamental skills signals where the future human premium will be paid.
