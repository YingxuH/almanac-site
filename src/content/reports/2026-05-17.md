### Today's Top Signals

**1. Mainstream Media Diagnoses the "AI Jobs Apocalypse"**
Both Bloomberg and The Economist published stark reports today detailing heavy job losses in roles heavily exposed to AI, noting that the tech industry is experiencing a severe bifurcation between the "haves and have-nots."
*Why it matters:* This transitions the narrative from theoretical displacement to measurable labor disruption. For the 3-10 year outlook, this accelerates the restructuring of engineering organizations into smaller, highly leveraged teams of senior reviewers managing fleets of automated agents, permanently dampening junior entry-level hiring.

**2. OpenAI Consolidates ChatGPT and Codex**
Following a leadership shakeup where co-founder Greg Brockman takes charge of product strategy, OpenAI is reportedly merging its conversational chatbot, ChatGPT, with its dedicated programming engine, Codex (TechCrunch). 
*Why it matters:* This signifies the end of standalone "coding-only" models in favor of unified, multimodal reasoning engines. It forces competitors to match a generalized, conversational interface for software development rather than building specialized IDE plugins.

**3. Academic Institutions Ban Zero-Touch AI Workflows**
ArXiv, the premier scientific research repository, announced a policy to ban authors for a year if they submit work entirely generated by large language models without human review (TechCrunch).
*Why it matters:* Echoing yesterday's warnings about corporate "AI psychosis" and Waymo's edge-case failures, this is a major institutional pushback against unverified automation. It enforces the necessity of "human-in-the-loop" workflows and suggests that fully autonomous code generation will face similar governance crackdowns in enterprise environments.

**4. OpenClaw's Unsustainable $1.3M Token Bill**
The creator of OpenClaw revealed a staggering $1.3M spend on OpenAI tokens in a single 30-day period (Signal via Twitter/steipete). 
*Why it matters:* This exposes the severe API cost traps associated with scaling wrappers around proprietary LLMs. It directly fuels the grassroots renaissance in self-hosted tooling and accelerates the migration toward sovereign, decentralized alternatives to avoid catastrophic cloud bills.

**5. Texas County Halts Data Center Construction**
A Texas county has passed a 1-year ban on new data center construction due to grid and resource strain (Politico).
*Why it matters:* The AI infrastructure layer is hitting hard physical limits. While AI chipmakers like Cerebras achieve massive $60B valuations, local municipalities are capping the compute expansion required to train and run next-generation coding models, potentially slowing the capability curve over the next 3 years.

**6. DeepSeek-V4-Flash Reignites LLM Steering**
Developer Sean Goedecke reports that the new DeepSeek-V4-Flash model has made low-level LLM steering techniques viable and interesting again.
*Why it matters:* As US proprietary models become more opaque and heavily guardrailed, developers are leveraging highly optimized, non-US models (like DeepSeek) to regain granular, low-level control over AI logic and outputs—deepening the market bifurcation between automated abstraction and developer autonomy.
